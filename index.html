What Is a Heart Murmur?

A heart murmur is the sound of blood moving through the heart, heard with a stethoscope. It is not an illness by itself—it is a sound that may or may not indicate a heart condition.

Many children develop what are called innocent murmurs during early growth. These murmurs are harmless, require no treatment, and are often outgrown.

Types of Murmurs

  • Innocent Murmur: Very common in children ages 3–7. No heart defect. No restrictions needed.
  • Abnormal Murmur: Less common. May indicate a valve or structural issue. Further testing is used to rule this out.

Why Doctors Might Recommend a Referral

Even if a murmur sounds innocent, doctors may refer your child to a pediatric cardiologist as a precaution. This ensures that there is no underlying issue and gives you peace of mind. An echocardiogram or electrocardiogram may be ordered to visualize the heart and confirm it’s functioning normally. This step is common, routine, and nothing to worry about.

Next Steps for Zoie

  • Echocardiogram (Echo): An ultrasound that visualizes heart structures.
  • Electrocardiogram (EKG): Measures the rhythm and electrical activity of the heart.
  • Pediatric Cardiology Referral: For expert evaluation if needed.

What Parents Should Know

Staying proactive and asking questions is the best approach. Zoie’s heart murmur is likely part of her growing body’s natural rhythm—but if it’s more than that, the family will be prepared and supported every step of the way.

Remember: most murmurs in children are not dangerous.
